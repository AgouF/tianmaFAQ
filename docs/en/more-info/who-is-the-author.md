### Authorship in the Digital Age

Traditionally, an author refers to the person who creates a literary, artistic, or scientific work and is the owner of the copyright. However, in the internet and AI era, the boundaries of "authorship" have become more blurred. For example, for content generated by AI, the "author" could be the human user who provided the instructions and requirements, the team that developed the AI model, or even the result of collaboration between both.

### How to Identify and Cite Author Information

When consulting and using any content, confirming author information is an important step, as it relates to the credibility of the information and copyright norms.

1.  **Look for Explicit Attribution**: Many websites, articles, or books will directly indicate the author's name, pen name, or institution name.
2.  **Check Copyright Notices**: Usually, in the copyright notice at the bottom of a webpage or the end of a document, you can find the rights holder.
3.  **Look for "About Us" Pages**: The "About" page of a website or platform (e.g., [☀️About](/about)) often explains the content producer and editorial team.
4.  **Assess the Nature of the Content**: For content generated by AI assistants or automated processes, platforms typically have relevant explanations indicating that it was created by AI and supervised and reviewed by a human team.
